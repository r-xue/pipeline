11 

12 The hifa_flagdata data performs basic flagging operations on a list of 

13 measurements including: 

14 

15 - applying online flags 

16 - applying a flagging template 

17 - partial polarization flagging 

18 - autocorrelation data flagging 

19 - shadowed antenna data flagging 

20 - scan-based flagging by intent or scan number 

21 - edge channel flagging, as needed 

22 - low atmospheric transmission flagging 

23 

24 About the spectral window edge channel flagging: 

25 

26 - For TDM spectral windows, a number of edge channels are always flagged, 

27 according to the ``fracspw`` and ``fracspwfps`` parameters (the latter 

28 operates only on spectral windows with 62, 124, or 248 channels). With the 

29 default setting of ``fracspw``, the number of channels flagged on each edge 

30 is 2, 4, or 8 for 64, 128, or 256-channel spectral windows, respectively. 

31 

32 - For most FDM spectral windows, no edge flagging is done. The only exceptions 

33 are ACA spectral windows that encroach too close to the baseband edge. 

34 Channels that lie closer to the baseband edge than the following values are 

35 flagged: 62.5, 40, 20, 10, and 5 MHz for spectral windows with bandwidths of 

36 1000, 500, 250, 125, and 62.5 MHz, respectively. A warning is generated in 

37 the weblog if flagging occurs due to proximity to the baseband edge. 

38 By definition, 2000 MHz spectral windows always encroach the baseband edge on 

39 both sides of the spectral window, and thus are always flagged on both sides 

40 in order to achieve 1875 MHz bandwidth (in effect, they are flagged by 

41 62.5 MHz on each side), and thus no warning is generated. 

42 

43 Returns: 

44 The results object for the pipeline task is returned. 

45 

46 Examples:

48
