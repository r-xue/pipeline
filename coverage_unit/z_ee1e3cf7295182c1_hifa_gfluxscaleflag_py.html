9 """Flag the flux, diffgain, phase calibrators and check source. 

10 

11 This task computes the flagging heuristics on the flux, diffgain, and phase 

12 calibrators and the check source, by calling hif_correctedampflag which 

13 looks for outlier visibility points by statistically examining the scalar 

14 difference of corrected amplitudes minus model amplitudes, and flags those 

15 outliers. The philosophy is that only outlier data points that have remained 

16 outliers after calibration will be flagged. The heuristic works equally well 

17 on resolved calibrators and point sources because it is not performing a 

18 vector difference, and thus is not sensitive to nulls in the flux density 

19 vs. uvdistance domain. Note that the phase of the data is not assessed. 

20 

21 In further detail, the workflow is as follows: a snapshot of the flagging 

22 state is preserved at the start, a preliminary phase and amplitude gaincal 

23 solution is solved and applied, the flagging heuristics are run and 

24 any outliers are marked for flagging, the flagging state is restored from the 

25 snapshot. If any outliers were found, then these are flagged. Plots are 

26 generated at two points in this workflow: after preliminary phase and 

27 amplitude calibration but before flagging heuristics are run, and after 

28 flagging heuristics have been run and applied. If no points were flagged, 

29 the 'after' plots are not generated or displayed. The score for this stage 

30 is the standard data flagging score, which depends on the fraction of data 

31 flagged. 

32 

33 The preliminary phase solutions use the mapping/combine and gaintype options 

34 as established in hifa_spwphaseup. 

35 

36 

37 Returns: 

38 The results object for the pipeline task is returned. 

39 

40 Examples: 

41 1. run with recommended settings to create flux scale calibration with flagging 

42 using recommended thresholds: 

43 

44 >>> hifa_gfluxscaleflag() 

45 

46 """
