6def hifa_bpsolint(vis=None, field=None, intent=None, spw=None, phaseupsnr=None, minphaseupints=None, evenbpints=None, 

7 bpsnr=None, minbpsnr=None, minbpnchan=None, hm_nantennas=None, maxfracflagged=None): 

8 """Compute optimal bandpass calibration solution intervals. 

9 

10 The task estimates the optimal time and frequency solution intervals needed to 

11 achieve the required signal-to-noise ratio. This estimation is based on nominal 

12 ALMA array characteristics and the observation's metadata. 

13 

14 The phaseup gain time and bandpass frequency intervals are determined as 

15 follows: 

16 

17 - For each data set the list of source(s) to use for bandpass solution 

18 signal-to-noise estimation is compiled based on the values of the 

19 ``field``, ``intent``, and ``spw`` parameters. 

20 

21 - Source fluxes are determined for each spw and source combination. 

22 

23 - Fluxes in Jy are derived from the pipeline context. 

24 

25 - Pipeline context fluxes are derived from the online flux calibrator 

26 catalog, the ASDM, or the user via the flux.csv file. 

27 

28 - If no fluxes are available the task terminates. 

29 

30 - Atmospheric calibration and observations scans are determined for each 

31 spw and source combination. 

32 

33 - If ``intent`` is set to 'PHASE' are there are no atmospheric scans 

34 associated with the 'PHASE' calibrator, 'TARGET' atmospheric scans will be 

35 used instead. 

36 

37 - If atmospheric scans cannot be associated with any of the spw and source 

38 combinations the task terminates. 

39 

40 - Science spws are mapped to atmospheric spws for each science spw and 

41 source combination. 

42 

43 - If mappings cannot be determined for any of the spws the task terminates. 

44 

45 - The median Tsys value for each atmospheric spw and source combination is 

46 determined from the SYSCAL table. Medians are computed first by channel, 

47 then by antenna, in order to reduce sensitivity to deviant values. 

48 

49 - The science spw parameters, exposure time(s), and integration time(s) are 

50 determined. 

51 

52 - The phase-up time interval, in time units and number of integrations 

53 required to meet the ``phaseupsnr`` are computed, along with the 

54 sensitivity in mJy and the signal-to-noise per integration. Nominal Tsys 

55 and sensitivity values per receiver band provided by the ALMA project are 

56 used for this estimate. 

57 

58 - Warnings are issued if estimated phase-up gain time solution would contain 

59 fewer than ``minphaseupints`` solutions. 

60 

61 - The frequency interval, in MHz and number of channels required to meet the 

62 ``bpsnr`` are computed, along with the per channel sensitivity in mJy and 

63 the per channel signal-to-noise. Nominal Tsys and sensitivity values per 

64 receiver band provided by the ALMA project are used for this estimate. 

65 

66 - Warnings are issued if estimated bandpass solution would contain fewer 

67 than ``minbpnchan`` solutions. 

68 

69 - If strong atmospheric features are detected in the Tsys spectrum for a 

70 given spw, the frequency interval of bandpass solution is recalculated to 

71 meet the lower threshold ``minbpsnr``, i.e., a lower snr is tolerated in 

72 order to preserve enough frequency intervals to capture the atmospheric 

73 line. 

74 

75 Returns: 

76 The results object for the pipeline task is returned. 

77 

78 Examples: 

79 1. Estimate the phaseup gain time interval and the bandpass frequency 

80 interval required to match the desired signal-to-noise for bandpass 

81 solutions: 

82 

83 >>> hifa_bpsolint() 

84 

85 """
